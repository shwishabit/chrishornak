Practice

Five Pennsylvania paths to a clearer record.

Every option we handle. Read the qualifier under each — if one sounds close, it's worth a free evaluation.

  • Governor's Pardon

    This fits if You have a felony or serious conviction and want it formally forgiven by the Governor of Pennsylvania.

    → Forgiveness recorded; most rights restored.

  • Record Expungement

    This fits if Your charges were dismissed or withdrawn, or you completed an ARD program, and you want them removed from your record entirely.

    → Charges erased from court and state records.

  • Record Sealing

    This fits if You have older convictions you can't expunge but want hidden from most background checks.

    → Hidden from employers, landlords, and the public.

  • Rights Restoration

    This fits if A conviction has cost you the right to vote, hold office, serve on a jury, or own a firearm, and you want those rights back.

    → Civic and firearm rights restored where eligible.

  • Cannabis Pardons

    This fits if You have a Pennsylvania marijuana possession conviction and want it pardoned under the Governor's expanded cannabis pardon program.

    → Expedited pardon under the cannabis program.

Plain answers

Questions worth asking first.

If your situation isn't covered here, send it through the free evaluation form below — we'll respond personally.

01Am I eligible to clear my record?+

That depends on the type of charge, when it happened, what the outcome was, and whether you've had any subsequent legal issues. Some clearances (like Pennsylvania's Clean Slate sealing) happen automatically; others require a petition. The fastest way to know is to send us your record details for a free evaluation. We'll tell you in plain language what's possible.

02What does this cost?+

Fees vary by service and complexity. Expungements and sealing petitions are generally less expensive than full Governor's pardons, which involve a much longer process. We'll quote a flat fee for your specific situation after the free evaluation. If cost is a barrier, ask us. Sometimes we can help find a path forward.

03How long does it take?+

Expungements and sealing can take a few months from filing to court order. Cannabis pardons under the Governor's expanded program move faster than full pardons. Standard Governor's pardons take years, often two to five, and require a hearing before the Board of Pardons. We'll give you an honest timeline once we know your specific case.

04Is what I tell you confidential?+

Yes. Anything you share with us during a consultation is protected by attorney-client privilege from the moment we begin discussing your case. We don't publish client names, share your information, or post about our cases. The work happens quietly.

05How do I know this is real?+

Attorney Dean A. Birdy has been practicing in Pennsylvania for over three decades, and PA Pardon has helped thousands clear their records. We are a Pennsylvania-licensed law firm, not a service or document preparer. You can verify Dean's bar status with the Pennsylvania Disciplinary Board. We'll never ask you to pay for anything before you understand exactly what we're doing for you.

06What if my conviction was a long time ago, or in another state?+

Older convictions are sometimes easier to clear, not harder. Pennsylvania's Clean Slate law specifically addresses older non-violent records. Out-of-state convictions are different. We only practice Pennsylvania law, so we can't clear records from other states. If your conviction was in another state, we'll point you toward who can.
