California Vignettes · March 2017

The Gold Bazaar

A morning at the Dubai gold bazaar — Svetlana shopping inside, me on a bench outside watching the street vendors work the sidewalk.

Incorrectly advised that all the shops would be open even though it was the morning of the Muslim sabbath, Svetlana and I took the twenty-minute cab ride from our hotel to the Dubai gold bazaar. Owing to the holiday, traffic was light and we actually wished the ride had taken a little longer so we would have had more time to admire the wonderful Arab buildings, each more modern and distinctive than its neighbor. The city was like a giant architectural competition. The income from the millions of tourists now visiting Dubai each year was indeed being put to spectacular use.

As we learned was usual in the Emirates, the driver barely spoke. And when he did, his English wasn't so good — on the first try, we mostly didn't understand him. Nevertheless, he was pleasant enough and probably because a lot of other tourists, we were told, were less generous tippers than Americans, he seemed excessively appreciative of his gratuity.

The city was like a giant architectural competition.
