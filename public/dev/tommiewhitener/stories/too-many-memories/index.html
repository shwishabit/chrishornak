Too Many Memories

By Tommie W. Whitener · January 2023

A son goes through his late father’s house, room by room, and learns how much of a life can be kept and how little of it explained.

He house had not been opened in three weeks, and still it did not smell of dust so much as of the particular way my father had lived in it — pipe tobacco from twenty years ago, the cedar of a closet he had built himself, and under all of it, faintly, the mint candies he kept in bowls on every flat surface.

My sister had flown in from Boston and spent the morning in the kitchen, which had always been her territory. I took the study, which had always been his. There were, I estimated, eleven thousand books. There were also the boxes.
