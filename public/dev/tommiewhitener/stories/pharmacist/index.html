The Pharmacist's Wife

By Tommie W. Whitener · June 1, 2022

With sincere apologies to Anton Chekhov.

He next night found the pharmacist's wife once again at the window. The street was quiet, save for the distant sound of a dog that had been barking for an hour, as if determined that someone, somewhere, would finally hear it and open a door.

She had not slept. She had not, in truth, slept well in years, though she would have struggled to pinpoint the week in which the trouble began. Her husband slept soundly beside her, as a man sleeps who has never examined his life too closely.
