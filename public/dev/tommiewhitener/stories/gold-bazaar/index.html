The Gold Bazaar

By Tommie W. Whitener · March 27, 2024

The gold market of Dubai on the morning of the Muslim sabbath — Svetlana and I, and the men on the benches.

Ncorrectly advised that all the shops would be open even though it was the morning of the Muslim sabbath, Svetlana and I took the twenty-minute cab ride from our hotel to the Dubai gold bazaar. Owing to the holiday, traffic was light and we actually wished the ride had taken a little longer so we would have had more time to admire the wonderful Arab buildings, each more modern and distinctive than its neighbor. The city was like a giant architectural competition.

As we learned was usual in the Emirates, the driver barely spoke. And when he did, his English wasn't so good — on the first try, we mostly didn't understand him. He was pleasant enough, and probably because a lot of other tourists were less generous tippers than Americans, he seemed excessively appreciative of his gratuity.

We had timed our trip to the Emirates perfectly, missing the winter cold by a few days and arriving before the onset of the unbearable summer heat. Thus I was quite comfortable as I sat in the warm air, enjoying strange fragrances emanating from the shops and observing the happenings. One of the first things I noticed was that there were several other men, no doubt tourists, sitting on other benches. They sat alone or in pairs, but periodically a woman or two would show up to drop off purchases or to exchange a few words with them — probably about possible deals, I guessed.
