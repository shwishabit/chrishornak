About the writer

Novato, California

Always trying to find the humorous, the ironic, even the perverse, I came to fiction late — after a long career in the law. Over forty-five years I split my practice between criminal defense and family law, which is to say I watched the worst people at their best and the best people at their worst. Among the writers I have sought to emulate are Anton Chekhov, Anthony Marra, and Joan Didion.

I did my undergraduate work at UCLA — my beloved City of the Angels — and then moved north to San Francisco for law school at UC Hastings. I have lived in Marin County for most of my adult life with my wife, Svetlana, but at heart I’m still an LA guy, and I expect a future novel or two to give prominence to the many wonderful characters I’ve known down there.

My stories draw on places and people I’ve actually known. Tom & Leone is a fictionalized account of my grandparents’ lives in rural Arkansas a century ago — one-armed Tom, mentally deranged Leone, and the four sons who somehow made normal lives. The Circassian Beauty follows Natalia and the cavalry officer Pavel across troika, train, and carriage from the untamed South to the salons of Czarist Russia; I speak Russian, and have wandered Moscow and Saint Petersburg enough to feel at home there too. California Vignettes was written for my own generation — the one that remembers Apollo 11 and still has stories to tell.

A quick review of my stories will show that I have written about one of my law clients (names and circumstances changed, of course), a hunting trip gone wrong, a trip to Dubai, a Chekhov riff, and weapons inside San Quentin. I have personally known all of these situations — well, maybe not a pharmacy in nineteenth-century Russia, but as noted, I do speak Russian.

I also served as editor of Endeavor: Stories of Struggle and Perseverance, the 2019 Redwood Writers anthology. Curation is a different skill than authorship, and a useful one — watching how disparate stories can form a cohesive whole has sharpened my own work.
