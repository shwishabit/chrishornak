Privacy

A plain-English summary of what this site does with your information. Short, because there isn’t much.

What this site collects

Two things, and only if you volunteer them:

  • Your email address, if you sign up for the newsletter. This goes straight to Substack, which handles delivery and list management.
  • Your name, email, and whatever you write in the message box, if you use the contact form. These are sent to Tommie so he can reply.

Nothing else — no tracking pixels, no advertising cookies, no analytics profiles tied to you.

Who else sees it

This site uses three third-party services. What they do and what they see:

  • Substack receives newsletter signups and sends the emails. See their privacy policy. You can unsubscribe from any Substack email in one click.
  • FormSubmit receives contact-form submissions and forwards them to Tommie’s inbox. See their privacy policy. They do not retain your message after forwarding.
  • Google Fonts serves the site’s typefaces. When your browser loads a page, Google may log the request. See their privacy policy.

What this site does not do

  • It doesn’t sell your data. No one is buying it.
  • It doesn’t share your address with anyone you didn’t intend to contact.
  • It doesn’t run third-party ads or behavioral trackers.

Your rights

You can unsubscribe from the newsletter from any email we send. You can ask Tommie to delete any message you sent via the contact form by writing to him. If you’re in California, Europe, or another jurisdiction that grants you specific data rights, those rights apply here too — email us and we’ll honor them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, the updated version replaces this one. The effective date below will move.

Effective April 23, 2026.
